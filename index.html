In the year 2030, experts predict that the population of Earth will reach 8.6 billion people. Simple math tells us, then, that approximately 17.2 billion eyes will also come to exist. Seventeen-point-two billion eyes that can look; 17.2 billion that can watch; 17.2 billion that can read, analyze, and process. But of those 17.2 billion, none but a select few will truly see. Only a few will enter this world with the gift of vision.

But who are these mythic individuals, those with the gift of sight? What do they do? And where on Earth do they hide?

This is the story of one house—eclectic, enigmatic, and eternal—where those with the gift of sight have gathered over the years, sharing their visions with the world in a farrago of leather and bamboo; of reds and greens; of dreams and fantasies. This is the story of the House of Gucci.

I

GUCCIO'S DREAM

Guccio Gucci watched from a distance as wealthy tourists, graceful and otherworldly, touted beautiful luxury suitcases to and fro. Although they passed within mere inches of him, they still somehow seemed as if they existed lightyears away.

An Italian immigrant working as a hotel attendant at the prestigious Savoy Hotel in London, young Guccio bore witness each and every day to the beauty that such garments and accessories could possess--and each and every day, his desire to create such beauty himself grew a little bit stronger.

II

GUCCI IS BORN

Inspired by the fineries he’d lusted after for so long, Guccio returned to his hometown of Florence, Italy, in 1902 to make his dream a reality.

In 1921, his eponymous brand came to life.

Gucci, a name now revered for its brilliance, sprung into humble beginnings.

III

BLOSSOMING

Employing local Florentine artisans, renowned for their craftsmanship, Guccio pieced together the brand’s image. As their fame grew, Guccio, along with his three sons, Aldo, Vasco, and Rodolfo, worked to expand their empire; new stores opened in Florence, as well as in Milan and Rome, throughout the early- to mid-20th century. City by city, year by year, people began

to take notice of Gucci’s fine leather handbags, suitcases, and shoes. During this time, many of Gucci’s most iconic creations came to exist: their brown and tan, diamond-patterned luggage; their handbags with bamboo handles; their beautifully-crafted leather loafers; and the green and red bands by which millions around the world still identify the brand to this day.

IV

INTERNATIONAL ICONS

Just after the conclusion of World War II, two of the Gucci sons, Aldo and Rodolfo, opened the family’s first store in New York City. As they networked with celebrities and socialites of the day, the American film stars of the 1950s also began vacationing more and more often in Florence, returning home with Gucci goods and establishing the brand as an international symbol of glamour and luxury.

Just fifteen days after the opening of the New York store, the family laid dear Guccio Gucci to rest at age 72. His eldest son, Aldo, rose to the head of the company and worked to fill his late father’s footsteps.

V

ETERNALLY REMEMBERED

As the decades passed, so did Gucci prosper. The 50s, 60s and 70s saw the brand thrive. Stores opened in London, Paris, Tokyo, Hong Kong, and Beverly Hills; icons like First Lady Jacqueline Kennedy and Princess Grace of Monaco inspired the Jackie bag and Flora print, two of Gucci’s most iconic creations.

By 1985, the accessory that perhaps champions as the brand’s most celebrated —the Gucci leather loafer—went up on display at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York, cementing the brand’s name as one to be eternally remembered.

VI

FAMILY FEUD

However, fame rarely comes without a price; and as Gucci’s international reputation continued to grow, as did its problems. It began with Maurizio Gucci, son of Rodolfo Gucci and heir to the throne, who took over the company from his dear

Uncle Aldo in 1984—only to surrender the remainder of the family’salready-halved shares to merchant bank InvestiCorp in 1985. After over 60 years of work, the family’s involvement in the brand came to an end.

Then, the unexpected. Standing in a doorway in Milan on one fateful day in March 1995, Maurizio Gucci met his untimely fate: gunned down by a hitman who his own wife hired to kill him.

VII

TOM TO THE RESCUE

By then, years of family feuds had taken their toll, and the mid-90s left Gucci in a dangerous place. Anxious investors on one side and a disappointed marketplace on the other, the decades-old fashion house desperately needed a rebranding and a hero after. Enter: Texas-born designer Tom Ford, appointed creative director in 1994.

His debut collection ablaze with edge and sex appeal, critics and fans praised the show for its brilliance and innovation. Several seasons in a row saw hit after hit under Ford’s care; the heavens opened, the angels sung, and the movie stars and fashion moguls flocked to the shelves. Risen from the ashes and born again, Gucci was back and better than ever.

IIX

90 YEARS OF GUCCI

In 2004, after ten years of work, Tom Ford left Gucci to form his own eponymous brand, leaving an open seat for the role of creative director that Frida Giannini would come to fill. Formerly a designer at Fendi, Giannini carried the brand through years of clean and crisp styles, more interested in the functional than the flashy.

During Giannini’s time, the brand hit another milestone: ninety years had already passed since Gucci was conceived. The opening of the Gucci Museo in Florence in 2011, inviting the world to take a peek back on nine decades of the house and its evolution in the same place where Guccio’s dreams first began to bloom.

IX

ENTER: ALESSANDRO

Just four years later, Gucci appointed a new captain to lead their ship to grander horizons. Born, raised and educated in Rome, creative director Alessandro Michele took hold of the wheel in January 2015, launching the house into a new era of unequivocal glory. With an eye for both the retro and the modern, Michele’s eccentric, maximalist taste turned the collective head of the industry and re-established Gucci as one of the most sought-after brands of the time.

Gucci straddles the fence between the absurd and outlandish and the romantic, all flaunted by specially-selected models who appear more fantastic or alien than real; loved by stars like Lana del Rey, Harry Styles and Michele-lookalike Jared Leto, the Hollywood glamour by which it made its name pronounces itself endlessly; and wielding the power of the internet in one hand, the brand holds the attention of the world in the other.

X

THE MUSIC PLAYS ON

To this day, crowds still look on as the glorious ‘GG’ symbol graces fashion weeks and runways everywhere. Under Michele’s eye, its garments carry a magic and whimsy with which few other brands can compete.

Were Guccio alive, his pride in the house he built would certainly remain. Even now, he watches from above as somewhere, another hotel attendant stands looking on, moved justly by the beauty of a Gucci bag in the hands of a attractive passerby.
